The Party Theme
How can large language models be effectively aligned with human values while maintaining their general capabilities?
This question sits at the intersection of AI safety, natural language processing, and ethics. As LLMs become more powerful and widely deployed, ensuring they behave in accordance with human intentions becomes increasingly critical.

Your Paper's Contribution
Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback
This paper proposes a novel approach to alignment that uses AI-generated feedback based on a set of principles (a "constitution") to train models, reducing reliance on human feedback while improving harmlessness.

Ouyang et al. (2022)
We agree that RLHF has limitations, but our InstructGPT results show it significantly improves model behavior. Perhaps the question isn't whether RLHF is "enough" alone, but how it can be combined with other techniques. Your constitutional approach could be a valuable complement.
Casper et al. (2023)
We'd strongly agree. Our survey identifies at least 12 fundamental challenges with RLHF, including reward hacking, inconsistent human preferences, and the difficulty of expressing complex values through pairwise comparisons. Constitutional principles could address the specification problem we highlight.
Christiano et al. (2017)
Interesting perspective. While our original framework focused on learning from human preferences, we always envisioned this as one component of a larger alignment strategy. How do you ensure the constitutional principles themselves are well-specified?
